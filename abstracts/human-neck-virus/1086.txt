Laryngeal mass with multiple cranial neuropathies as a presenting sign for
varicella zoster infection.

Shihada R(1), Brodsky A, Luntz M.

Author information: 
(1)Department of Otolaryngology-Head and Neck Surgery, Bnai-Zion Medical Center, 
Technion-Israel Institute of Technology, Haifa, Israel.
rabia.shihada@b-zion.org.il

The most common presentation of varicella zoster virus (VZV) infection is
unilateral distribution of herpetic eruptions and neuralgia. Laryngeal
involvement is considered very rare.
